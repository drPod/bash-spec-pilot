I used nix for POSIX user/group/chroot-related primitives and tempfile for sudoedit's temporary copies. The implementation covers common option parsing, command execution, shell/login modes, list/validate/version/help, timeouts, chdir/chroot when permitted, environment assignments, and a pragmatic sudoedit; it intentionally skips real sudoers authentication/plugins, SELinux contexts, ACL/xattr handling, PAM/session logging, and full pty/signal forwarding.
